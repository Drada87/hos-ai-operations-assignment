RAW INPUT — TASK 2 (FLIGHT FINDER TOOL)
Trip request from the founder. Build a tool that takes a brief like this and returns the best itinerary.

----------------------------------------------------------------
TRIP BRIEF
----------------------------------------------------------------
Traveler: the founder (1 passenger)
Home airport: San Francisco (SFO). OAK or SJC acceptable if it clearly beats SFO.

This is a MULTI-CITY trip next month (July):

  Leg 1 — SFO -> Boston (BOS)
    Depart: Mon Jul 13
    HARD CONSTRAINT: must land in Boston no later than 5:00pm local
    (there is a 7:00pm dinner with the pilot partner that cannot be missed).

  Leg 2 — Boston (BOS) -> New York City (JFK or LGA, EWR acceptable)
    Travel: Wed Jul 15, after 3:00pm (the summit ends at 2:00pm; needs ~1hr buffer to reach the airport).
    Must be in NYC by end of day Jul 15.

  Leg 3 — New York -> SFO (home)
    Depart: Fri Jul 17
    PREFERENCES: not a red-eye; must land at SFO before midnight Jul 17.

----------------------------------------------------------------
PREFERENCES & SCORING (these intentionally conflict — the tool must trade them off)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Budget: keep total airfare under $1,500 if at all possible.
  BUT the founder will pay up to $400 more to avoid a red-eye OR to save 3+ hours of total travel time.
- Nonstop strongly preferred. A connection is OK only if it saves >$250 or is the only option.
- Layovers: no connection shorter than 45 min or longer than 2.5 hrs.
- Airline: mild preference for United or its partners (founder has status) — worth a small bonus, NOT a hard rule.
- Fare class: NO basic economy (founder wants a seat assignment + to keep miles).
- Bags: carry-on only. Seat: aisle preferred.
- Time-of-day: dislikes departures before 7:00am.

----------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT THE TOOL SHOULD OUTPUT
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. A single recommended end-to-end itinerary (all 3 legs) with airline, flight #s, times, layovers, fare class, and TOTAL price + TOTAL travel time.
2. A clear, ranked runner-up, and one sentence on why the recommendation beat it.
3. An explicit note of any constraint that could not be satisfied and the tradeoff made.
4. Show your scoring logic (how price vs. time vs. red-eye vs. airline preference were weighted).

Use real/live fare data if you can reach it. If you can't, clearly state your data source/approach
and use realistic sample fares — but the SCORING and SELECTION logic must be real and reusable for a
different trip brief.
